### Case Study: The "Balance Test" Viral Engine
*   **The Problem:** How do you sell an invisible technology?
*   **The Solution:** A 30-second "Push Test" that anyone can perform.
*   **The Mechanics:**
    *   **0-10s:** Subject stands on one leg. Tester pushes them. They fall (instability).
    *   **10-20s:** Apply the patch. Wait 30 seconds.
    *   **20-30s:** Push them again. They don't fall.
*   **The Result:** The subject's *own body* becomes the testimonial.
*   **Viral Factor:** Everyone who sees the test wants to try it. Everyone who tries it wants to share it.
*   **Key Insight:** "If you can make the customer sell themselves, you don't need to sell."

### The D.E.M.O. Framework

| Step | Action | Example (Service Business) |
|------|--------|---------------------------|
| **D - Demonstrate** | Show the "before" state (the problem). | "Let me look at your current website. See how the call-to-action is buried below the fold?" |
| **E - Explain** | Briefly explain what you'll change. | "We're going to move this button and change the copy." |
| **M - Magnify** | Amplify the impact of the change. | "This one change typically increases conversions by 20-30%." |
| **O - Overcome** | Address the unspoken objection. | "And it takes us about 15 minutes to implement. No disruption to your site." |

| **Agency** | Instant fix: "Let me rewrite this one headline right now. Watch the difference." |
| **SaaS** | Live demo: "Let me show you how this saves you 2 hours per week." |

### Case Study: Super Patch Pricing Strategy
*   **The Research:** Survey data showed $40-60 was the "willingness to pay" range.
*   **The Decision:** Price at the *higher end* ($49.95 per patch) to maintain premium positioning.
*   **The Margin Math:**
    *   Target Price: $50
    *   Target Margin: 70%
    *   Allowable COGS: $15
*   **The Result:** If manufacturing cost exceeded $15, they would have killed the product.
*   **Key Insight:** "We didn't set the price based on cost. We set the price based on what the market would pay, then engineered the cost to fit."

### The 3-Tier Offer Stack Template

| Tier | Name | Price Range | Includes | Best For |
|------|------|-------------|----------|----------|
| **1: Entry** | "Starter" / "Essentials" | $X - $XX | Core deliverable only. Self-serve. | Price-sensitive. Testing the waters. |
| **2: Core** | "Professional" / "Growth" | $XX - $XXX | Core + Support + Community. | The majority of clients. Best value. |
| **3: Premium** | "VIP" / "Enterprise" | $XXX - $XXXX | Core + Support + Community + 1-on-1 Access + Custom. | High-touch clients. Highest margin. |

### Pricing Psychology Principles

| Principle | Description | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **Anchoring** | Show the highest price first. | "The VIP is $10,000. The Professional is $2,500." |
| **Decoy Effect** | Make the middle option look like the best value. | Add features to Tier 2 that make Tier 1 look too basic. |
| **Price-Value Disconnect** | Price based on *value delivered*, not hours worked. | "This audit will save you $100k/year. It's $5,000." |
| **Urgency (Real)** | Create genuine scarcity. | "I only take 5 new clients per quarter." |

### Case Study: Super Patch's CapEx Innovation
*   **The Problem:** Scaling mass media (billboards, TV, Super Bowl) requires millions in upfront capital.
*   **The Innovation:** "CapEx" tokens — distributors buy $50-$500 tokens that fund specific ad campaigns.
*   **The Mechanics:**
    *   Distributor buys tokens → Funds pooled → Company runs ads → Leads distributed back to token holders.
*   **The Result:** $5M+ in advertising funded without corporate cash outlay.
*   **Key Insight:** "Our distributors became our investors, and our investors became our best salespeople."

### The LTV Optimization Levers

| Lever | Tactic | Impact on LTV |
|-------|--------|---------------|
| **1. Onboarding** | Send "Quick Start" guide within 24 hours. | +15-20% 90-day retention. |

| **3. Cross-Selling** | Recommend complementary products. | +10-15% order value. |
| **4. Loyalty Programs** | Points-based rewards for repeat purchases. | +20-30% repeat rate. |
| **5. Proactive Support** | "Check-in" email at Day 30. | -10-15% churn. |
| **6. Re-Engagement** | 4-email sequence for at-risk customers. | +5-10% winback rate. |

### The Re-Engagement Sequence

| Day | Trigger | Message | Offer |
|-----|---------|---------|-------|
| **45** | No reorder in 45 days. | "We miss you!" | 15% off next order. |
| **60** | Still no reorder. | "Is everything okay?" | Personal check-in. |
| **75** | Still no reorder. | "Last chance before we close your file." | 25% off. |
| **90** | Still no reorder. | "We're here when you're ready." | Pause account. Archive. |
