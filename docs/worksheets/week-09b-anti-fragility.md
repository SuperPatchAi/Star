## Part 1: Understanding Anti-Fragility

### The Fragility Spectrum

| State | Definition | Response to Stress | Example |
|-------|------------|-------------------|---------|
| **Fragile** | Breaks under stress | Worse | Glass vase |
| **Resilient** | Withstands stress | Same | Rubber ball |
| **Anti-Fragile** | Gets stronger from stress | Better | Human muscle |

### Business Translation

| State | Business Response to Crisis | Example |
|-------|----------------------------|---------|
| **Fragile** | Fails, shuts down, loses customers | Single-product company in recession |
| **Resilient** | Survives, maintains, holds steady | Diversified company in recession |
| **Anti-Fragile** | Thrives, gains market share, acquires | Company with cash reserves in recession |

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|

| **What's the worst case if this fails?** | |
| **Do we have a backup plan?** | |
| **Are we concentrating or diversifying risk?** | |

### Decision Types:

| Decision Type | Anti-Fragile Approach |
|---------------|----------------------|
| New customer (huge) | Ensure they're <20% of revenue |
| New supplier | Keep alternatives, don't over-commit |
| Debt | Maintain ability to survive 12+ months without revenue |
